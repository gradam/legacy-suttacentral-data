Ekottarāgama 21.4

“If there are persons towards whom you cultivate friendliness (maitrī) in your hearts, who think they can really trust in you and who listen to you respectfully, viz. father and mother, brothers, family members, relatives, friends or inmates, you should establish them in three bases (sthāna) on which they should take their stand unwaveringly. Which are the three?

“They should be encouraged (sam-ut-thā, √sthā) unwaveringly to take delight (prīti) in the Tathāgata thus: He is the Tathāgata who has realized absolute truth (paramārthāgata),2 the Fully and Completely Enlightened One, perfect in knowledge and conduct, the Blessed One, Knower of the universe, the Incomparable One (anuttarapuruṣa), the Teaching Guide to show sentient beings the Way (mārgadharmasārathi),3 the Teacher of Gods and Men, the Buddha, the Exalted One.

“Again, they should be encouraged to direct their attention to the Teachings thus: The Tathāgata’s Dharma is well proclaimed, inviting (aihipaśyika),4 in the highest degree (paramataḥ) making for exquisiteness (sūkṣmatā), yielding immediate results (sāṃdṛṣṭika)5 and thus to be understood by the wise (vedanīyo vijñaiḥ).6

“Similarly, they should be encouraged to contemplate and honour (√man) this Order of the Disciples, the Tathāgata’s Śrāvakasaṃgha, thus: Among them prevails absolute concord (sāmagrī),7 they walk in the right path (nyāyapratipanna),8 are endowed with all virtues9 and have achieved perfection in moral training, concentration and wisdom, in ultimate freedom (vimukti) and insight-knowledge of this freedom, that is to say the Order of Disciples, the four pairs of noble persons and the eight kinds of āryas.10 This is the Tathāgata’s Śrāvakasaṃgha, worthy of respect (gauravārha) and worthy of veneration;11 this is the world’s incomparable field of merit (puṇyakṣetra). Whoever, bhikṣus, trains in these three bases will achieve great results (phalavipaka). Thus, bhikṣus, you should train.”

After listening to the Buddha’s words, the bhikṣus were pleased and respectfully applied themselves to practice.12
