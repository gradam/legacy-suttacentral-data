Theravāda Vinayapiṭaka

Monks’ rules and their analysis

Monks’ Suspension 4: Attending to one’s own desires

Origin story

BD.1.222Bu-Ss.4.1.1At one time the Buddha, the Master, was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park. At that time Venerable Udāyī associated with and visited a number of families. Then, having dressed in the morning, Venerable Udāyī took his bowl and robe and went to the house of a beautiful widow, Vin.3.132where he sat down on the prepared seat. That woman approached Venerable Udāyī, paid homage to him and sat down to one side. Venerable Udāyī instructed, inspired and gladdened her with a Dhamma talk. She then said, “Bhante, please say what you need. I am able to give robes, almsfood, lodging requisites, and medicines.”

“It’s not hard for us, sister, to get those requisites. Give instead what is hard for us to get.”

“What is that, bhante?”

“Sexual intercourse.”

“Is it needed now, bhante?”

“It is, sister.”

“Come, bhante,” and she entered her bedroom, took off her clothes, and lay back on the BD.1.223bed. Venerable Udāyī approached her and spat out, “Who would touch this foul-smelling wretch?” and he departed.

Then that woman complained and criticised him: “These Sakyan recluses are shameless, immoral, and liars. … To be expanded as in Bu-Ss.2.1.1. … How can the ascetic Udāyī request sexual intercourse of me, and then spit out, “Who would touch this foul-smelling wretch?” and depart? What is bad about me? How am I foul-smelling? In what way am I inferior to whom?”

Other women, too, criticised him in the same way, Bu-Ss.4.1.2and the monks heard them. The monks of few desires … complained and criticised him: BD.1.224“How can Venerable Udāyī, in the presence of a woman, praise attending to his own desires?”

Those monks rebuked Venerable Udāyī in many ways and then informed the Master. And the Master convened the Sangha Vin.3.133and questioned Venerable Udāyī:

“Is it true, Udāyī, that you praised attending to your own desires in the presence of a woman?”

“It’s true, Master.”

The Buddha, the Master, rebuked him: “It’s not suitable, foolish man, it’s not becoming, it’s not proper, it’s not worthy of a recluse, it’s not allowable, it’s not to be done. How could you, in the presence of a woman, praise attending to your own desires? Have I not taught the Dhamma in many ways for the sake of dispassion, not for the sake of passion … for the stilling of the fevers of sense desire? This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“If a monk, lustful and with a distorted mind, in the presence of a woman praises attending to his own desires: ‘Sister, she provides the highest service who attends in this way to a virtuous one like me, BD.1.225 a celibate, one of good character,’ and if it is a reference to sexual intercourse, he commits an offence entailing suspension.”

Definitions

Bu-Ss.4.2A: whoever … Monk: … The monk ordained by a complete Sangha through a procedure of one motion and three announcements, which is unchallengeable and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.

Lustful: having lust, longing for, in love with.

With a distorted mind: a mind with desire is distorted, a mind with anger is distorted, a confused mind is distorted. But in this case distorted refers to a mind with desire.

Woman: a human female, not a female spirit, not a female ghost, not a female animal. She understands and is capable of discerning bad speech and good speech, what is lewd and what is not lewd.

In the presence of a woman: near a woman, close to a woman.

His own desires: one’s own sense desires, for the sake of oneself, aiming at oneself, service to oneself.

The highest: this is the highest, this is the best, this is the foremost, this is the utmost, this is the most excellent.

She: a noble woman, a brahmin woman, a merchant-caste woman, or a low-caste woman.

One like me: a noble man, a brahmin, a merchant-caste man, or a low-caste man.

Virtuous: one who refrains from killing living beings, who refrains from stealing, who refrains from lying.

A celibate: one who refrains from sexual intercourse.

One of good character: he is one of good character because of that virtue and because of being celibate.

BD.1.226In this way: with sexual intercourse.

Attends … to: gives pleasure to.

If it is a reference to sexual intercourse: if it is a reference to the sexual act.

He commits an offence entailing suspension: … This is the name and designation of this class of offence. Therefore, too, it is said that he commits an offence entailing suspension.Vin.3.134

Permutations

Bu-Ss.4.3.1It is a woman, he perceives her as a woman, and he has lust. If the monk, in the woman’s presence, praises attending to his own desires, he commits an offence entailing suspension.

It is a woman, but he has doubts about it … This paragraph is to be expanded as in Bu-Ss.2.3.1. … but he perceives her as a paṇḍaka … but he perceives her as a man … but he perceives her as an animal, and he has lust. If the monk, in the woman’s presence, praises attending to his own desires, he commits a grave offence.

It is a paṇḍaka, he perceives him as a paṇḍaka, and he has lust. If the monk, in the paṇḍaka’s presence, praises attending to his own desires, he commits a grave offence.

It is a paṇḍaka, but he has doubts about it … This paragraph is to be expanded as in Bu-Ss.2.3.1. … but he perceives him as a man … but he perceives him as an animal … but he perceives him as a woman, and he has lust. If the monk, in the paṇḍaka’s presence, praises attending to his own desires, he commits an offence of bad conduct.

It is a man … This paragraph is to be expanded as in Bu-Ss.2.3.1. … it is an animal, he perceives it as an animal … but he has doubts about it … but he perceives it as a woman … but he perceives it as a paṇḍaka … but he perceives it as a man, and he has lust. If the monk, in the animal’s presence, praises attending to his own desires, he commits an offence of bad conduct.

It is two women, he perceives them both as women, and he has lust. If the monk, in the presence of both women, praises attending to his own desires, he commits two offences entailing suspension. … To be expanded as in Bu-Ss.2.3.2. …

It is a woman and a paṇḍaka, he perceives both as women, and he has lust. If the monk, in the presence of both of them, praises attending to his own desires, he commits one offence entailing suspension and one offence of bad conduct. … To be expanded as in Bu-Ss.2.3.3. …

Non-offences

Bu-Ss.4.3.2There is no offence: if he says, “Attend on us with robes, almsfood, lodging requisites, and medicines;” if he is insane; if he is the first offender.

Case rulings

Mnemonic list

How may a barren woman, can she get a son,
Dear to, may I be fortunate;
What may I give, with what may I be of service,
How may I get a good rebirth.

Case details

Bu-Ss.4.4.1At one time a certain barren woman said to a monk who was supported by her family: “Bhante, how may I become pregnant?”

BD.1.227“Well, sister, give the highest gift.”

“What is the highest gift, bhante?”

“Sexual intercourse.”

He became anxious … “Monk, you have committed an offence entailing suspension.”

Bu-Ss.4.4.2At one time a certain fertile woman said to a monk who was supported by her family: “Bhante, how can I get a son?”

“Well, sister, give the highest gift.” …

“Monk, you have committed an offence entailing suspension.”

Bu-Ss.4.4.3At one time a certain woman said to a monk supported by her family: “Bhante, how can I become dear to my husband?"

“Well, sister, give the highest gift.” …

“Monk, you have committed an offence entailing suspension.”

At one time a certain woman said to a monk supported by her family: “Bhante, how may I become fortunate?”

“Well, sister, give the highest gift.” …

“Monk, you have committed an offence entailing suspension.”

Bu-Ss.4.4.4At one time a certain woman said to a monk supported by her family: “Bhante, what can I give you?”

“The highest gift, sister.” …

“Monk, you have committed an offence entailing suspension.”

Bu-Ss.4.4.5At one time a certain woman said to a monk supported by her family: “Bhante, how can I be of service?”

“With the highest gift, sister.” …

“Monk, you have committed an offence entailing suspension.”

Bu-Ss.4.4.6At one time a certain woman said to a monk supported by her family: BD.1.228“Bhante, how may I get a good rebirth?”

“Well, sister, give the highest gift.”

“What is the highest gift, bhante?”

“Sexual intercourse.”

He became anxious and thought, “The Master has laid down a training rule. Could it be that I have committed an offence entailing suspension?” He informed the Master.

“Monk, you have committed an offence entailing suspension.”

The fourth training rule on attending to one’s own desires is finished.
